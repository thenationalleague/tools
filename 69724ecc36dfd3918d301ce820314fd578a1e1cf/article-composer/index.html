Editor

A weekend bursting with individual quality brought hat-tricks, thunderbolts, inspired debuts and late-match turning points across all three divisions.

From relentless finishers to standout full-backs and creative sparks, these were the players who shaped the latest round of National League football.

Braintree Town goalie Jack Thompson earns his spot for the Enterprise National League after a smart, composed display, producing several sharp stops to secure a 1-0 home win over Morecambe.

At the back, Sutton United’s Junior Eccleston was outstanding at both ends in their 4-2 win over Gateshead, getting forward to assist the third goal. Woking’s Caleb Richards delivered an impressive defensive showing in the 2-0 victory over Brackley, while Rochdale centre-half Ethan Ebanks-Landell brought calm assurance in their 2-0 win against Eastleigh.

In midfield, Hartlepool’s Jamie Miley capped an excellent display with a thunderbolt to seal a 3-1 home win over Truro. Boreham Wood’s Erico Sousa was at his creative best, curling in a beautiful assist before slamming home the second in their 2-1 victory against Halifax. Altrincham’s Matty Kosylo was central to a dramatic turnaround against Scunthorpe - assisting the first and scoring the late equaliser before his side earned a 4-2 comeback win. Yeovil Town’s Luke McCormick struck both goals in their important 2-1 win over Boston, while Forest Green’s Harry Whitwell, a West Brom loanee, levelled with a superb strike as Rovers overturned Southend 2-1 for his overdue first goal for the club.

Up front, York City’s Joe Grey produced a blistering second-half hat-trick as they swept aside Aldershot 5-1, while Carlisle United’s Georgie Kelly struck both goals in a well-earned 2-1 home win over Tamworth.

In the Enterprise National League North, Worksop Town’s Lewis Forshaw claims the goalkeeper’s spot after a brilliant debut on loan from Burnley, making a string of crucial saves in the 2-1 win at Leamington.

Teammate Hamza Bencherif joins him for his game-winning contribution as he turned in a corner from close range. AFC Fylde’s Lincoln McFayden shone on his return to the squad, assisting twice in a confident 3-1 victory at Curzon Ashton. Macclesfield’s Sam Heathcote was defensively solid and fizzed in the opener during their 1-1 draw at Scarborough.

In midfield, Darlington’s Will McGowan was the standout performer in their dramatic 4-3 win at Bedford Town, contributing a composed finish and driving the game from midfield. Merthyr Town’s Lewys Twamley blasted in a 30-yard strike in their 2-1 win at Buxton, while Scarborough’s Noah Wadsworth supplied the crucial assist for the leveller in their 1-1 home draw with Macclesfield. Chester’s Pat Jones had already scored and assisted to put them 2-0 up at Spennymoor before the match was abandoned due to waterlogging.

Up front, Radcliffe’s David Ball won a penalty and executed a delightful chip as part of a lively 2-2 draw with Telford. King’s Lynn striker Adam Marriott delivered the decisive finish in a tight 1-0 win away at Oxford City, while Kidderminster’s Michael Gyasi, on loan from King's Lynn, seized on a mistake to score his first goal for Harriers in a 3-1 victory over Chorley.

Farnborough stopper Jack Turner produced an outstanding display in the Enterprise National League South, keeping his side in the game repeatedly until they eventually nicked a 1-1 draw against Ebbsfleet.

Worthing’s Toby Byron starred at left-back, scoring the third and assisting the fourth in an emphatic 5-0 win against high-flying Hornchurch. Dover’s Jalen Jones was dominant in their 2-0 win over Slough, even helping create the opener when his header rebounded off the bar before being tucked in. Torquay’s Jordan Thomas completes the defensive picks after a superb showing in their 2-0 win at Chelmsford, helping send United top of the table.

In midfield, Chesham United’s Alex Babos reacted quickest to loose play to score the opener in a 2-1 home win over Dorking. Maidstone’s TJ Bramble produced a moment of brilliance with a match-winning overhead kick in their 1-0 win over Salisbury. Hemel Hempstead’s Finley Wilkinson inspired a second-half turnaround at Tonbridge, equalising and contributing heavily to their 3-1 recovery. Maidenhead’s Liam Dulson shone in Alan Devonshire’s final match in charge, delivering a fine display and the opening goal in a 3-0 win over Chippenham.

Up front, Dagenham & Redbridge’s Andy Carroll rolled back the years with a first hat-trick in nearly a decade - all inside a 25-minute spell - as the Daggers crushed Totton 5-0. Eastbourne Borough’s Pemi Aderoju took the spotlight with four goals in a 6-0 demolition of Hampton & Richmond. Weston-super-Mare’s Scott Wilson rounds off the forward line after an all-action performance in the 1-1 draw at in-form Horsham, striking the post and assisting the equaliser.
